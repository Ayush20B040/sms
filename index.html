Steel Making Process

The steel-making process involves several key steps:

  • Ironmaking: Raw materials like iron ore, coke, and limestone are converted into molten iron in a blast furnace.
  • Basic Oxygen Steelmaking: Molten iron is converted into steel by blowing oxygen through it in a basic oxygen furnace.
  • Secondary Metallurgy: The steel is further refined to achieve the desired chemical composition and temperature.
  • Casting: The molten steel is solidified into semi-finished products like billets, blooms, or slabs.
  • Hot Rolling: The semi-finished products are rolled at high temperatures to produce finished products like sheets, plates, and bars.
  • Cold Rolling: The hot-rolled products are further processed at room temperature to achieve precise dimensions and surface finishes.
